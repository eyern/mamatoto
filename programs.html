Our Events/Programs

Mama Toto Funday (Every 2nd Saturday of the month)

Step back into the golden days of your childhood with our unique event. This event is designed to transport you back to a time of pure joy and boundless imagination. Whether you’re looking to relive your favorite childhood memories or experience a day of carefree fun, this event promises an unforgettable journey through the magic of yesteryears. Highlights of the Day includes childhood Games , Compete in friendly matches, enjoying childhood treats such as sweets, biscuits & ice-creams, sing-along to nursery rhymes, dancing to hit music of yester years, a bonfire as you listen to storytelling sessions featuring beloved fairy tales that bring your favorite childhood characters. The event is setup near a gently flowing river and a green surroundings. The soothing sounds of the water and rustling leaves create a peaceful ambiance, perfect for unwinding and reconnecting with nature. Join us for a day filled with laughter, creativity, and play. It's time to rekindle your inner child and experience the wonder all over again!

Mom-to-Be & New Mom Meetup (Every 1st Saturday of the Month)

Join us for our Monthly Meetup, a nurturing space where expectant and new mothers come together to share, learn, and grow. Each month, we offer a blend of engaging activities, expert-led discussions on parenting and baby care, and a chance to connect with other moms in a relaxed, supportive environment. This event is open to expectant ladies and those with babies less than 1 year. It's a baby friendly environment and one can comfortably come with her baby. Enjoy refreshments, gain valuable insights, and form lasting friendships as we celebrate the journey of motherhood together. Whether you're anticipating your baby’s arrival or adjusting to life with your newborn, our community welcomes you with open arms. Come be a part of our monthly tradition of joy, support, and shared experiences.

Mama Toto Book Club ( Every 3rd Saturday of the month)

Join our warm and welcoming Ladies' Book Club, where stories come alive and friendships bloom! Whether you’re an avid reader or just looking to explore new genres, our book club offers a cozy space to share insights, laughter, and meaningful discussions over a cup of tea or coffee. We meet monthly, on every 3rd Saturday, where we discuss a diverse selection of books, ranging from contemporary fiction and timeless classics to thought-provoking non-fiction. Come for the books, stay for the camaraderie!

Dress Up & Show Up Event (Every 4th Saturday of a month)

A "Dress Up & Show Up" event is a dazzling affair beckoning attendees to embrace the art of style and presence. Its a dress-themed event offering an opportunity to infuse creativity and a sense of celebration. The best dressed lady is rewarded for her efforts. Its an afternoon of enjoying High tea, featuring a delightful assortment of teas, served alongside an array of delicate pastries in a serene and sophisticated ambiance. Attendes savor, not only exquisite flavors but also celebrate the art of conversation and the pleasure of taking a moment to relax and enjoy the finer things in life. What a better way to crown our high tea, than to have our own Ted Talk? We listen to short encouraging stories from our own, aimed at creating a shared experience that promotes a sense of community and belonging, reinforcing the idea that we are not alone in our struggles and aspirations.

5 Reasons Why Women’s Mental Health is Important

  • Women suffer from higher rates of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD), depression, and anxiety. These experiences can have long-lasting effects on mental health and require specialized treatment.
  • Hormonal changes and reproductive health can also impact women’s mental health. Conditions such as premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD) and postpartum depression can affect women during certain stages of life, requiring specialized treatment and support.
  • Women have unique biological factors that can impact their mental health, including hormonal fluctuations during the menstrual cycle, pregnancy, and menopause.
  • Societal expectations and pressures can also affect women’s mental health. Women are often expected to juggle multiple roles, including caregiver, breadwinner, and homemaker, which can be stressful and overwhelming.
  • Lastly, intersectionality and the impact of social identities, including race, ethnicity, sexuality, and ability, can significantly affect women’s mental health.
